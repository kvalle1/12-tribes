# Tribe Index — Assessment Design

## Concept

The Tribe Index assessment identifies which of the 12 biblical tribes best
matches how a person is wired — their strengths, shadow, and the conditions
under which they thrive or fall.

It is built in two layers:

**Self Assessment** — How you see yourself.
You select words from a flat list of adjectives that describe you. No grouping,
no obvious categories. Just words. The scoring behind the scenes maps your
selections to tribe profiles and identifies your primary and secondary tribe.

**360 Observer Assessment** — How others see you.
After completing your own assessment, you receive a shareable link. Send it to
3–5 people — a manager, peer, spouse, coach, or mentor. They see the same word
list with the prompt: *"Select the words that best describe [Name]."*

The report shows both results side by side. Where they align, you have
confirmation. Where they diverge, you have data — the gap between how you see
yourself and how others experience you is often where the most important growth
lives.

---

## Assessment Flow

```
1. User enters name + email
2. User completes word selection (self)
3. User receives results + shareable observer link
4. Observers complete word selection (for user)
5. Full report unlocks: self vs. others comparison
```

## What We Need Feedback On

1. **The words themselves** — Do they feel accurate to each tribe's profile? Anything missing or misplaced?
2. **Overlap decisions** — Where a word maps to two tribes, does that feel right or forced?
3. **Coverage balance** — Dan and Issachar have the most words. Naphtali and Simeon have the fewest. Does that match the complexity of each profile?
4. **Missing words** — What adjectives would you add to the list that aren't here?
5. **Words to cut** — Anything that feels too niche, too religious, or won't resonate in a corporate context?
