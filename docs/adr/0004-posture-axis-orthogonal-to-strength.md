# Posture is a first-class axis, orthogonal to strength

Every scored Marker contributes to two things: the tribe's **strength** (is this
wiring present?) and its **Posture** (where on the fall→oil arc — from
active-shadow to integrated). Posture is aggregated per tribe and surfaced in the
report. Markers are drawn from all four profile fields (`strength`, `oil`,
`shadow`, `fallLine`) and tagged with their type.

This exists to resolve a confound: a person wired for a tribe may have *matured
past* its fall-line and will resist or contradict the pattern. Reading
absence-of-fall-line as absence-of-tribe would systematically under-score the most
self-aware participants.

## The rule that a future reader will find surprising

A fall-line Marker fires on **resonance with the theme, not on current
dysfunction.** Maturity around a fall-line is evidence *of* the tribe (you don't
build hard-won resistance to a tendency that was never yours). So:

- A matured fall-line **never lowers** tribe strength. It moves Posture toward
  *integrated* and routes the evidence to the tribe's `oil` Marker, which
  cross-confirms the attribution via a different Marker type.
- The agent distinguishes *integrated* (matured, real) from *not-resonant*
  (genuinely not this tribe) by probing for the **felt internal logic** of the
  tendency, not the behavior — first-person emotional texture counts; third-person
  conceptual description does not.

## Consequences

- The report can place the participant on each tribe's redemption arc, aligning
  with the `status` field already in `tribes.ts`.
- Scoring logic must treat fall-line/shadow Markers as additive to strength;
  anyone "fixing" them to subtract is reintroducing the confound this ADR exists
  to prevent.
