# Scoring is status-blind; status drives framing only

All 12 tribes are eligible as Primary in the Interview, including the ones flagged
`disqualified` (Dan, Simeon), `disqualified-arc` (Gad), and `warning` (Reuben).
The `status` field affects only report framing and interpretation, where it
couples to Posture — it never gates scoring.

A tribe's `status` describes its *fate in the biblical narrative*, not whether its
wiring exists in living people. Dan-wiring (vigilance/strategy/discernment) and
Simeon-wiring (conviction/zeal/justice) are real and common. Barring disqualified
tribes from Primary would mis-attribute a genuinely Dan-wired person to their
second tribe — exactly the inaccuracy this instrument exists to remove. So the
scoring gate stays open to all 12; status belongs in interpretation.

## Consequences

- A "your Primary is Simeon (disqualified)" result is valid and needs careful,
  non-condemning report copy. That is a writing problem, not a scoring one, and
  Posture (active-shadow = cautionary, integrated = redemption) gives the copy its
  hook. The content already carries redeemed exemplars (Nehemiah for Dan, Simeon
  of Luke 2, Paul for Benjamin).
