# Funnel: bounded-broad Calibration, then information-gain Discrimination

The Interview selects questions in two phases. **Calibration**: a short, fixed
opening of broad "feeler" questions that touch all 12 tribes' territory at low
resolution. **Discrimination**: information-gain questioning that targets whatever
best separates the live Contenders.

We chose this over pure information-gain from question one (narrows from the first
answer, so that answer disproportionately steers everything — an anchoring bias
that reintroduces the exact problem this instrument exists to remove) and over a
fully fixed phase script (predictable but can't chase the surgical distinctions
that justify using an agent).

## Two guards a future reader will not infer from "information gain"

1. **Demote-the-leader, not confirm-it.** Discrimination targets separating the
   whole live Contender set, and preferentially asks questions that could *demote*
   the current leader or *promote* a dark horse. Naive greedy info-gain that keeps
   probing the leader makes the rich get richer and must be avoided.
2. **Neutral phrasing.** When narrowing to "Judah vs Benjamin," the agent asks
   situation/behavior prompts that let Markers emerge unprompted — never leading
   questions ("do you like being in charge?"). Narrowing must not become coaching.

## Consequences

- The fixed Calibration opening is an anti-anchoring floor, not just structure;
  shortening it to "save questions" trades away bias protection.
