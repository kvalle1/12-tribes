# Answer modality: free-text default, targeted Dilemmas

Participants answer in free-text prose by default. During Discrimination, the
agent may deploy a **Dilemma** — a forced-choice between two concrete,
tribe-unlabelled situations — to break a stubborn tie between Contenders.

Free-text is the engine: it produces unprompted Markers and the emotional texture
the felt-logic and Posture mechanics need. Pure guided choice was rejected because
pre-mapped options reintroduce both the leading-question problem and the word
list's self-flattery bias, and cap surgical depth. Dilemmas are kept as a narrow
tie-breaker because a forced trade-off is bias-resistant in a way free text is
not — you cannot claim both.

## Guard a future reader must preserve

A Dilemma's options carry **no tribe labels**, and the scored signal is the
*trade-off plus the free-text "why"*, not the bare option picked. Mapping options
directly to tribe scores would collapse the Dilemma back into the guided-choice
failure mode this design rejects.

## Consequences

- Two turn types exist (free-text question, Dilemma); the turn schema must carry
  the type and the dilemma's situations.
- A small bank of tribe-pair Dilemmas (or agent-generated ones constrained to the
  Marker Catalog) must be authored alongside the catalog.
